Vaccination

All those who care for animals, including pet owners and farmers have a duty to protect the health and welfare of animals under their care.

CAT

Your kitten will require two series of vaccines to get started, the first at nine weeks and the second at three months. After that, kittens and cats usually require 'booster' immunizations once a year.

BIRD

Polyomavirus vaccine is commercially marketed. Some avian doctors advise initiating vaccination at 21 days of age and maintaining the vaccine every two weeks. They then adhere to the instruction that the bird take the immunization once a year.

BIRD
DOG

Vaccines for canine parvovirus, distemper, canine hepatitis, and rabies are considered core vaccines for dogs. Non-core vaccines are administered based on the dog's exposure risk. Among these are vaccinations against Bordetella bronchiseptica, Borrelia burgdorferi.
